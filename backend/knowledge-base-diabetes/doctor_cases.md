## Case 01 | 54yo Female | BMI 33.4 | Stage 3 | All Risk Factors Present

**Patient Profile:**
- Age: 54 | Gender: Female | Height: 158 cm | Weight: 83 kg | BMI: 33.4
- Waist Circumference: 97 cm (above female threshold of 88 cm)
- Diet Food Habits Score: 8.2 (Very Poor)
- Blood Pressure: Yes | Cholesterol/Lipid Levels: Yes | Vision Changes: Yes

**Clinical Assessment:**
This patient presents with multiple compounding risk factors: class I obesity, severe central adiposity, hypertension, dyslipidaemia, and visual disturbances — all occurring simultaneously at age 54. This constellation strongly indicates metabolic syndrome and very high imminent risk of Type 2 Diabetes onset or existing undiagnosed diabetes.

**Doctor Recommendation:**

1. **Immediate Referral:** Refer urgently to an endocrinologist and ophthalmologist. Vision changes at this risk level may represent early diabetic retinopathy or hyperglycaemia-induced lens changes. Do not delay.

2. **Blood Work — Urgent:**
   - Fasting plasma glucose
   - HbA1c (target <7% if diabetic, <5.7% if pre-diabetic)
   - Full lipid panel (LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
   - Kidney function (eGFR, urine albumin-creatinine ratio)

3. **Diet — Immediate Intervention:**
   - Eliminate all sugar-sweetened beverages, white rice (replace with red/brown rice or kurakkan), processed foods, and deep-fried items
   - Adopt Mediterranean dietary pattern: olive oil, vegetables, legumes, fish, limited red meat
   - Daily caloric target: 1400–1600 kcal/day with dietitian supervision
   - Sodium restriction: <1500 mg/day (critical for BP control)

4. **Physical Activity:**
   - Begin with 20-minute walks twice daily, progressing to 150 min/week moderate aerobic exercise
   - Add resistance training 2x/week after 4 weeks (improves insulin sensitivity by 10–15%)

5. **Weight Target:** 5% body weight reduction in 3 months (≈4 kg) — proven to reduce diabetes incidence by 58%

6. **Blood Pressure Management:** Target <130/80 mmHg. DASH diet + medication review with physician.

7. **Monitoring:** Weekly weight, fortnightly BP, HbA1c every 3 months.

**Scientific Basis:**
UKPDS (1998): Tight BP control reduces diabetes-related deaths by 32%. DPP (2002): 5–7% weight loss reduces T2D incidence by 58%.
